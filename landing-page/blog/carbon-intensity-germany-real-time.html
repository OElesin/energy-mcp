Real-Time Carbon Intensity of Electricity in Germany

Not all kilowatt-hours are created equal. At noon on a sunny, windy day, German electricity can be under 150 gCO₂/kWh — cleaner than France's nuclear-heavy grid. At 6 PM on a calm winter evening, it can spike above 500 gCO₂/kWh as gas and coal plants ramp up to meet demand.

If you care about reducing your carbon footprint — or just want to run your EV, heat pump, or washing machine on the cleanest electricity — you need to know when the grid is green.

What Is Carbon Intensity?

Carbon intensity measures how many grams of CO₂ are emitted for each kilowatt-hour of electricity generated. It depends on the generation mix — what percentage comes from solar, wind, gas, coal, nuclear, and other sources.

Source gCO₂/kWh Type
Wind (onshore/offshore)11Renewable
Nuclear12Low-carbon
Hydro24Renewable
Solar45Renewable
Biomass230Renewable
Natural gas490Fossil
Oil650Fossil
Coal (hard coal + lignite)820Fossil

Germany's grid-average carbon intensity typically ranges between 200–500 gCO₂/kWh, depending on the time of day, weather, and season. In 2025, the annual average was approximately 350 gCO₂/kWh.

When Is the Grid Greenest?

In Germany, the patterns are predictable:

  • Midday (10:00–15:00): Solar peaks. On sunny days, carbon intensity can drop below 200 gCO₂/kWh.
  • Night with wind (00:00–06:00): If wind is strong, overnight carbon intensity stays low despite no solar.
  • Evening peak (17:00–20:00): Solar drops, demand rises, gas plants ramp up. Typically the dirtiest hours.
  • Weekends: Lower industrial demand means less fossil generation needed.

The key insight: the cheapest hours and the greenest hours often overlap. When renewables are abundant, supply exceeds demand, driving prices down AND carbon intensity down. This means optimizing for cost often automatically optimizes for carbon too.

Use in Home Assistant

Track carbon intensity as a sensor and use it in automations:

command_line:
  - sensor:
      name: grid_carbon_intensity
      command: >
        curl -s -X POST https://energy-mcp.getbrechtai.com/mcp
        -H "Content-Type: application/json"
        -H "Accept: application/json, text/event-stream"
        -d '{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":1,"method":"tools/call","params":{"name":"get_carbon_intensity","arguments":{"country":"DE"}}}'
        | grep "^data:" | sed 's/^data: //'
      value_template: >
        {{ (value_json.result.content[0].text | from_json).carbon_intensity_gco2_kwh | round(0) }}
      unit_of_measurement: "gCO₂/kWh"
      scan_interval: 1800

Automation: Run Washing Machine Only When Grid Is Green

automation:
  - alias: "Notify when grid is green"
    trigger:
      - platform: numeric_state
        entity_id: sensor.grid_carbon_intensity
        below: 200
    condition:
      - condition: time
        after: "08:00:00"

    action:
      - service: notify.mobile_app
        data:
          title: "🌱 Grid is green right now"
          message: >
            Carbon intensity: {{ states('sensor.grid_carbon_intensity') }} gCO₂/kWh.
            Good time to run the washing machine or dishwasher!

Multi-Country Support

Carbon intensity varies dramatically across Europe. Energy MCP supports all major European countries:

  • France (FR): ~60 gCO₂/kWh (nuclear-dominated)
  • Germany (DE): ~200–500 gCO₂/kWh (weather-dependent)
  • Poland (PL): ~600–800 gCO₂/kWh (coal-heavy)
  • Norway (NO): ~20 gCO₂/kWh (almost 100% hydro)
  • Netherlands (NL): ~300–450 gCO₂/kWh (gas-heavy)

Change the country parameter to any supported code: DE, FR, NL, BE, AT, ES, PT, IT, CH, PL, CZ, DK, NO, SE, FI, EE, LV, LT, HU, RO, BG, GR, IE, GB.
